The Deathclock

9/21/24

If I don't land some sort of art job before next year, I will be working full-time in a labor job. I am fine with this and I'm actually pretty certain I'll be happy. Still, I feel that clock ticking. When my time runs out, I won't be able to dedicate myself to my work as often.

Reflecting on this past year, I've wasted a lot of time. I have plenty ideas that I'd love to bring to life, but I've spent to much of my time taking it easy. Well, it's more complicated than that. I have to take things easy to some degree. That's what I do to stay alive. But, I really should dedicate myself more to what I want to do. And I haven't been doing that for a while. I think I just feel obligated to serve people, and I don't treat myself in a way that is respectful to myself. Lately, serving people has meant being there for them. It's hard to push against that. The feeling that if you weren't there, things would be worse. The feeling that my actions can make someone's life worse. And knowing that the actions I want to take, directly compete with the happiness of others.

I think I'd like to stop acting that way. I want to stop seeing things from that perspective. I want to do what I want to do. I haven't felt that in a long time, but now that I feel it, I can't just let it go. So, how has this manifested itself?
