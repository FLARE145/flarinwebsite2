Working Full-Time

6/3/26

One year ago, I started working at my current job. Many aspects of my life changed, and one of them was the amount of time I work. I worked part-time for 3 years, and had never worked actual full time until I started here. I had worked 8 hour shifts, but never 5 back-to-back 8 hour shifts. That change makes a big difference in the overall feel of my life.

So, after a year, how am I feeling? I'm feeling great to be honest. I think I just lived my most mentally stable year of the past five. Working more has made it much harder to get my own projects down, but I'm still doing a lot. It's just at my workplace and not at home.

For anyone actually reading this, I'd like to remind you that you don't know what the future holds for you. I genuinely gave up. And I'm not where I specifically wanted to be. But before two years ago, I didn't even know that I job like mine existed, and now I have it. If you feel like you don't really know what you want to do, or that you're dreams are impossible, consider that what you end up doing might not be something you're currently aware about.

The point of life is not work anyways. Probably. Like it's totally fine to work and have a job you hate. My point is more that you shouldn't dread being miserable at a future job since you don't actually know how you will feel once you're there.

I'm super grateful for what I have, and I know it's only because of luck. I wish everyone the same luck, and I wish the global economy a, "please, get well soon."
