Things I'd like to buy but shouldn't

4/29/26

Valve just announced the price for their new Steam Controller. $100. There is mostly no reason for me to get one, but my financial situation would allow me to... But I won't.

I feel like I've been attacked by a variety of good advertising lately. For example, the new Framework laptop looks so good. I think they did actually make the perfect laptop. For $2000. It would be so awesome to use but I do have a decent laptop already. It's just no longer ideal for me. Maybe I can sell it to justify getting a new one. But I really really don't need it. No matter how much the advertising makes it feel like a smart idea to buy one.

An oddly similar one is the Slate truck. I literally already have a vehicle that works, but I like the idea of having an EV. And I think it looks nice. And it's easily customizable. And the creators of it care about right to repair. It genuinely is likable for exactly the same reasons a Framework seems cool. The only thing the vehicle is missing is Linux at this point.

A weird aspect of my problem here is that I feel the need to support products I think are good, and I'm scared that by not buying them now, there won't be products of that nature for me to buy in the future because the companies went under. This is very dumb logic obviously.

Oh yeah on top of all of those, I keep looking at prices for manufactured homes. I like the idea of actually living on my own but again, I can't really justify it. I just get caught up in hype and fantasy but I gotta stay grounded.

The nice thing is that when I decide to not buy any of these, I get to just pocket all the money I would've spent on that stuff. So that's good. I'm happy I was raised to be responsible with money. At least I think I am responsible. I could probably be more responsible.
