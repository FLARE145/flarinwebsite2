The Amazing Digital Circus

6/6/26

Yesterday, I had the pleasure of watching the finale to The Amazing Digital Circus in the theater. It was so good that I'm still "recovering." (P.S. this post is spoiler free other than a brief mention of episode 4)

I don't remember how much I've talked about this show on my blog, but over the past ~2.5 years, it has become an oddly significant part of my life. What I mean is that I remember waiting for every episode, and I remember the all the ways my life changed during the show's run. Even if I wasn't an "active member of the fandom," I cared about the show enough that it is intertwined with my memory of the past few years.

I really enjoyed TADC. I remember when I watched the first episode with my brother, we discussed the show's potential for what must have been at least an hour. I was very into animating at the time, and I sent a cold email to Glitch Productions offering my skills. I never received a response, but even just forcing myself to do that was one the first steps to me really coming into my own. Later on, I found a number of the themes and topics very relatable to my own life. My favorite episode is Fast Food Masquerade, in which an artist throws themselves deeply into work to distract from unrealized dreams. As a retail worker at the time, I heavily related to what was presented on screen. The feeling of leaving work and looking up at the stary sky, wondering how you ended up here instead of elsewhere. Wondering why you're own emotional instabilities feel halfway meant for the world, and halfway counter to it.

I really could talk about this show for a long time. So much so that I feel overwhelmed to do so. It is a genuinely good show, and if you'd like to talk about it with me personally, that would be preferable to me attempting to write a review. However, I do have some quick thoughts about the movie release I'd like to mention.

I was initially worried that the finale to the show would not make for a good theater experience because I know it was not going to be action-heavy. This episode was going to be a strange experience because it's been a strange show. The quality of the show has always come in part from it's character focus, and I just didn't think a bunch of character talking to each other would make for a good movie. Thankfully, I was proven wrong. The episode has such a strong emotional core (aided by the soundtrack) that I felt it was a great movie experience. I'm very happy I went to go see it with my friends. TADC didn't need fancy action to carry it on the big screen. It justifies its presence with intense emotion, and it was a refreshing watch for that reason. With such a strong ending for the series, I can officially say that the show reached that potential I and many others saw since the pilot episode. Congratulations to Gooseworx, Glitch Productions, and everyone else involved in the production of this show. Sometime last year (or maybe two years ago?) I began to donate to Glitch in the form of $10 monthly Glitch Inn subscription. I do this because I love art and animation, and I've been regularly impressed by what these people are bringing to the table. This is the most I can currently do to support creativity in the world, and I'm happy that I get to be proud of this. Thank you everyone for making something that means so much.

I'm excited to re-watch the finale when it comes out in two weeks on youtube, and I will watch it multiple times over as I have with every episode. In the words of one of the silliest gifs I've seen (iykyk), I love you guys, we are TADC
