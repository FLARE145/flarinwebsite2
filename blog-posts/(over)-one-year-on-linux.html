(Over) One Year on Linux

6/11/26

Judging by a previous blog post, I’ve been using Linux on my desktop pc for over a year now. I’m estimating the day I switched from Windows 10 to OpenSUSE Tumbleweed to be 5/23/25, so I’m roughly three weeks late to writing this post. I currently have a dual-boot setup, but I haven’t booted into windows for a very long time now. If I had to guess, it’s probably been over 6 months since I last booted windows on this computer for any reason. I still have win10 on a laptop I sometimes use though.

So what’s it been like? Genuinely blissful. I get home excited to get on my computer, and it has never felt more like my computer. I do miss being able to use certain software more easily, but the cost has been worth it to me. Even the cost of all the troubleshooting I’ve had to do. I definitely ran into a lot of problems throughout my first real year on linux, but everytime I solve a problem, I learn something. I think I know more about how my computer works than I ever did on windows. I don’t know how to say this without it sounding dumb, but using linux actually makes you smarter. Or, at the very least you get a little more patient. A lot more knowledgeable. I find it a little difficult to recommend to someone who needs their computer for work, and isn’t ready to troubleshoot computer issues. If you are in my shoes (has a job that doesn’t utilize my personal computer and am interested in tech), you are the perfect person to switch to linux. Make that sacrafice. Whatever software isn’t available on linux, let it go. Windows is just, so gross in comparison. Even more now than a year ago.

It’s a crazy thing to be excited for computer updates. KDE Plasma has a new update soon that I’m excited for. My computer keeps getting better. When I originally switched, my nvidia gpu was causing a lot of issues on wayland, but it works so much better now. If you have an nvidia gpu, I honestly think linux is completely viable now. Although, I highly recommend setting up snapshots to recover from in case things get unstable. OpenSUSE comes with Snapper, which has saved my life countless times. I also recommend actually following forums. I only started doing this recently and it’s been a massive help to be aware of current issues, and to learn about how those issues came to be.

This past year has also been good proof that even an employed person can figure out how to use a computer. Some problems had to be solved over the course of weeks because of my time management, but everything always gets solved, and it’s very rewarding. A lot of people wouldn’t even call me smart or “good at computers” but I’m doing just fine. It’s mostly your passion that matters I think.

I really think the fabled “year of the linux desktop” was last year. I mean, it’s the year I switched over so that makes sense, right? It did really feel like a turning point though. Many people I know use linux and it makes me feel comfortable. To close this post out, I leave you with a screenshot of my current desktop, see ya
