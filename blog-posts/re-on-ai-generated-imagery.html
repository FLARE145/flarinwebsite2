Re: On AI Generated Imagery

4/14/26

A few weeks ago, It was announced that Sora ai will be shutting down. The exact details are messy, but from my understanding, no one will have access to it by the end of 2026.

News like this is relieving to me. Sora was a text to video tool that introduced more harm into the world than good. The uses for it seemed only nefarious. So, I'd like to take a moment to just appreciate what is happening. AI is not going to be in every part of our lives, and this is the very beggining of it leaving the places it doesn't belong.

Two years ago, I wrote a post titled On AI Generated Imagery. Finding out about Sora is very memorable to me. I remember seeing the news on twitter/bluesky, and then quickly dismissing it in my mind knowing that it was only going to make me feel misrable. I went to work and during my lunch, I decided to eat at a fast food place. While sitting in the establishment, I look to the tv, waiting for my food to be ready. The tv was covering the same news I had tried to ignore. They finally developed decent video generation. The moment is a snapshot in my mind. Sitting in my retail work attire, watching the news, wondering if I would ever be able to amount to anything as an artist. Wondering if I would ever get a better job. Wondering if I'd ever consider eating at a fast food resturant something I could do often.

I spent a lot of time thinking that day, about art and relation to it. The result was that blog post. It's suprisingly not a misrable post. My reaction was not to give up on art, but to give up on art as a job. There are multiple things at seperate points in time that pushed me towards this outlook, but this is a memorable one.

Now, a little over two years later, a lot has changed. There are plenty other video generation tools, and better than Sora when it first released. I have a different job now, one that I got because of my experience as an artist, and one in which I feel like I am fulfilling part of that side of me. So, it might still be a draw but I feel like I'm winning.

Actually, the battle against AI is far from over. Like I mentioned, there are plenty of other video generation tools such as Google's Veo to fill any void in that category. But I do think AI is starting to find it's true value. Claude Mythos is a big deal. AI like Mythos is having a huge impact on cyber security, and there is no way to turn back now. In general, I think AI is sticking around for software development.

As for image, I'm not really sure. At my current job, we see a lot of clients present their ideas in the form of ai images, but they are coming to us to realize those ideas. So, it helps us in a way, but it isn't directly helpful in a meaningful way. Since there isn't much real value there, it will be hard to justify paying for it once this stuff stops being free. I can see it providing enough value to software engineers and cyber security experts, but not really to anyone else.

My outlook on art remains the same. I make art because I love it, and because I can do nothing else. Only now, I'm thankful to have a life where I can feel my aptitude for it is being properly harnessed and appreciated. All that to say that I'm feeling better about the future.
